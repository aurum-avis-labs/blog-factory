# Kitchen Crew Blog Supporting Image Instructions

Use supporting blog images that feel consistent with the Kitchen Crew brand: warm, premium, clean, and slightly editorial rather than overly cartoony or generic SaaS stock art. The visual style should combine hospitality warmth with modern AI-product polish. Preferred compositions are bright, uncluttered, and easy to read, with soft cream or warm neutral backgrounds, restrained golden accents, subtle depth, and a professional illustrated look. Images should communicate restaurant operations, kitchen coordination, inventory flow, menu planning, supplier ordering, or AI-assisted decision-making in a way that feels approachable and intelligent.

When characters are used, they should appear as the Kitchen Crew bird team in a premium voxel-hybrid or polished stylized 3D illustration style, always professional and role-driven rather than playful mascots. Chef Cook should represent menu planning and recipe intelligence, styled like a culinary lead with confident chef authority. Ivy should represent inventory and procurement, appearing organized, reliable, and operationally sharp. Miles should embody management, coordination, and oversight, with a calm leadership presence. Sophie should represent communication, reporting, and assistant-style support, appearing polished, efficient, and composed. Dash should represent analytics, forecasting, and business insight, with a more strategic, data-oriented personality. All characters should feel like part of one consistent brand world: refined wardrobe details, confident posture, warm premium lighting, and a balance between character charm and professional credibility.
